# Family Prepared App — Usage Guide

A step-by-step guide for using the Family Prepared app on your phone or computer.
No technical knowledge required.

## What This App Does

Family Prepared helps your family create and maintain an emergency preparedness plan.
It works completely offline (no internet needed after install) and stores your data
privately on your device. You can back it up to Google Drive and share it with family.

## Installing the App

### On iPhone / iPad

1. Open Safari and go to: `https://jpwill00.github.io/family-prepared-app/`
2. Tap the Share button (square with arrow pointing up)
3. Scroll down and tap **"Add to Home Screen"**
4. Tap **Add**
5. The app icon appears on your home screen — tap it to open

### On Android

1. Open Chrome and go to: `https://jpwill00.github.io/family-prepared-app/`
2. Tap the three-dot menu (top right)
3. Tap **"Add to Home Screen"** or **"Install app"**
4. Tap **Install**
5. The app appears in your app drawer

### On Computer (Chrome, Edge, etc.)

1. Go to: `https://jpwill00.github.io/family-prepared-app/`
2. Click the install icon in the address bar (or use the browser menu → "Install app")
3. The app opens in its own window and works offline

## First Run

When you first open the app, it loads a reference library with 10 emergency
preparedness topics (first aid, communications, water, food, shelter, evacuation,
documents, power, pets, and mental health). These are read-only reference materials
you can browse anytime.

## Building Your Plan

Use the sidebar (or hamburger menu on mobile) to navigate between sections:

### Plan Sections

| Section | What to fill in |
|---------|----------------|
| **Household** | Family members, address, medical notes, dietary needs, pets |
| **Communications** | How you'll reach each other (primary, backup, radio, emergency) |
| **Evacuation** | Routes out of the area, meeting points, emergency contacts |
| **Inventory** | Go-bag contents, medications, water supply, food, equipment |
| **Documents** | Where important papers are stored (passports, insurance, etc.) |
| **Utilities** | How to shut off water, gas, and electricity at your home |

### Tips for Filling In Your Plan

- **Start with Household and Communications** — these are most critical in an emergency
- **Be specific**: "meter key on tool wall in garage" is better than "in the garage"
- **Include phone numbers**: write them out even if they're in your contacts (your phone might be dead)
- **Update quarterly**: medications expire, phone numbers change, kids grow up

## Reading the Library

The Library section contains reference articles on emergency preparedness topics:

- First Aid (bleeding control, burns, CPR basics)
- Communications (PACE planning, radio basics)
- Water (purification, storage)
- Food (emergency supply, power outage food safety)
- Shelter (emergency heat, shelter-in-place)
- Evacuation (planning, go-bag packing)
- Documents (what to copy and where to store)
- Power (backup options, generator safety)
- Pets (emergency kit for animals)
- Mental Health (disaster stress management)

These are read-only. If you want to add your own notes, use "Fork to edit" which
copies the article to your Custom section where you can modify it.

## Offline Use

This app works completely without internet once installed. Your data is stored on
your device. You can:

- View your entire plan offline
- Edit any section offline
- Browse all library articles offline

Changes are saved locally. When you reconnect, you can sync to Drive.

## Setting a Passphrase

For sensitive information (SSN, medical details), you can set a passphrase:

1. Go to **Settings**
2. Under **Security**, enter a passphrase
3. Sensitive fields are now encrypted on your device

If you forget your passphrase, encrypted fields cannot be recovered. Choose something
memorable but not guessable.

## Backing Up to Google Drive

See [Sharing with Family](sharing-with-family.md) for the full Drive setup guide.

Quick version:
1. Go to **Settings**
2. Tap **Connect Google Drive**
3. Authorize with your Google account
4. Tap **Snapshot Now** to back up
